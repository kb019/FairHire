# Demo Script

## Goal

This demo shows how Ethics Hiring Tracker reduces bias at two points in the hiring funnel:

- when a role is written
- when candidates are reviewed

## Suggested Demo Length

5 to 7 minutes

## Demo Story

“This product is designed around a simple idea: fairness should be built into the hiring workflow, not added later as a manual audit. The system helps HR write cleaner job postings, keeps early candidate review anonymous, and only reveals applicant identity when a recruiter deliberately requests it.”

## Step-By-Step Walkthrough

### 1. Show the home page

What to say:

“Ethics Hiring Tracker is a full hiring workflow, not just a classifier. It covers job creation, applicant intake, and anonymous candidate review.”

### 2. Register or sign in as HR

What to show:

- HR auth flow
- entry into the HR workspace

What to say:

“The HR side owns job posting creation, analytics, and candidate review.”

### 3. Open the job posting editor

What to show:

- role details
- live review rail
- compliance score

What to say:

“The editor runs live bias analysis while the posting is being drafted. This pushes fairness upstream, before candidates ever see the role.”

### 4. Enter intentionally problematic text

Suggested examples:

- gender-coded language
- age-coded language
- unrealistic experience requirements
- no inclusive statement

What to say:

“The system flags concrete issues, explains why they matter, and suggests a rewrite. It also maintains a compliance score over time.”

### 5. Save the posting

What to show:

- successful save
- HR dashboard
- posting list
- analytics widgets

What to say:

“The posting, score, and ethics report are persisted. HR can then review issue patterns and score trends across roles.”

### 6. Switch to the applicant experience

What to show:

- applicant registration or sign-in
- applicant portal
- full posting details

What to say:

“Applicants do not just see a role title. They see the actual job description, department, location, and compensation before they apply.”

### 7. Upload a resume

What to show:

- resume upload
- submission confirmation
- application tracker

What to say:

“The resume is parsed into structured sections and then redacted before HR review. The applicant still gets a normal application-tracking experience.”

### 8. Return to HR candidate review

What to show:

- candidate list
- anonymous ID
- fit snapshot
- structured experience and skills

What to say:

“This is the key product moment: HR can review the candidate without seeing direct contact details or institution names. The system preserves relevant hiring signal while limiting identity signal.”

### 9. Reveal contact information

What to show:

- contact reveal action
- disclosed contact details

What to say:

“Identity is not removed forever. It is gated. A recruiter can request contact details, but that is a deliberate, logged step rather than the default.”

## Closing

Suggested close:

“The main contribution of this project is not a single model. It is the workflow design. The system makes fairer behavior easier at job creation time and more accountable at review time.”
